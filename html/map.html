India Gate

India gate was constructed to honor the soldiers of India that were killed during world war 1. This structure boasts of a beautiful architecture by India. It was first inaugurated in the year 1931 and it still has the same charm.

  • Type: War Memorial
  • India Gate Opened on: February 12, 1931
  • India Gate Height: 42 m
  • Address: Rajpath, India Gate, New Delhi, Delhi 110001
  • Architect of India Gate: Edwin Lutyens
  • Formerly Called: Kingsway
  • Location of India Gate: Rajpath
  • Nearest Metro Station to India Gate: Central Secretariat
  • Timings of India Gate: Open all day (24x7), Monday to Sunday
  • Entry Fee: Free entry
  • Phone No: 011 2464 7005

Note: India gate Boating timings are from 2 PM to 9 PM.

Timings of light show at India Gate are from 7 PM to 9.30 PM

Qutub Minar

Qutub Minar is the second tallest and ever attractive historical monument of India located in Delhi at Aurobindo Marg, Mehrauli. It is built in unique architectural style using red sandstones and marble. It is considered that Mughals built this victory tower to celebrate their victories over the Rajputs.

  • Type: Monument
  • Qutub Minar Height: 72.5 meters
  • Location: Mehrauli, New Delhi, Delhi, 110030, India
  • Qutub Minar Built by: Qutub-ud-din Aibak.
  • Nearest Metro Station: Qutub Minar Metro Station
  • Timings of Qutub Minar: Monday to Sunday, 7:00 am - 5:00 pm
  • Entry Fee:
    • 30 per person for Indians
    • 0 per child (below 15 Years)
    • 500 per person for Foreign Tourists
    • 25 for Still Camera (non-commercial use)
    • 25 for Video Camera (non-commercial use)
  • Phone No: 011 2336 5358

Lotus Temple

The Lotus Temple in New Delhi is one of 7 temples of the Baha'i faith in the world. Like all Baha'i temples its structure is a circular 9-sided shape. The building is made up of 27 free-standing marble petals arranged to form the 9 sides of the temple.

  • Type: Expressionist architecture
  • Lotus Temple Built on: November 13, 1986
  • Lotus Temple Height: 34 meters
  • Address: Lotus Temple Rd, Shambhu Dayal Bagh, Bahapur,
    Kalkaji, New Delhi, Delhi 110019
  • Architect of Lotus Temple : Fariborz Sahba
  • Material used: White marble
  • Location of Lotus Temple: Near Kalkaji Temple, East of Nehru Place
  • Nearest Metro Station to India Gate: Kalkaji Mandir
  • Timings of India Gate: Tuesday to Sunday, 9:00 am - 7:00 pm (Summer), 9:00 am - 5:30 pm (Winter)
  • Entry Fee: Free entry
  • Phone No: 011 2644 4029

Red Fort

The Red Fort is considered one of the most significant monuments of India, built by the Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan. It showcases a splendid display of architecture made of marble works and red sandstones. The construction for the Lal Quila started in 1638. The Red Fort is a famous tourist place in India.

  • Type: Monument
  • Built in: mid 17th century
  • Red Fort Area: Spread over an area of about 256 acres
  • Built by: Shah Jahan
  • Material used: red sandstones
  • Location of Red Fort: Netaji Subhash Marg, Near Chandni Chowk
  • Nearest Metro Station to Red Fort: Chandni Chowk Metro Station
  • Timings of Red Fort: Tuesday to Sunday, 9:30 am - 4:30 pm
  • Address: Netaji Subhash Marg, Chandni Chowk, New Delhi, Delhi, 110006, India
  • Entry Fee:
    • 35 per person for Indians

Jantar Mantar

Built in 1724 by Maharaja Jai Singh II of Jaipur, Jantar Mantar is one of the five astronomical observatories built by the king in Northern India. Its striking combinations of geometric forms have caught the attention of architects, artists and art historians from around the world.

  • Type: Astronomical Observatory
  • Built in: 1724
  • Built by: Maharaja Jai Singh II of Jaipur
  • Material used: red stones and marbles
  • Location of Jantar Mantar: Sansad Marg, Near Connaught Place
  • Nearest Metro Station to Jantar Mantar: Patel Chowk
  • Timings of Jantar Mantar: Monday to Sunday, 6:00 am - 6:00 pm
  • Address: Sansad Marg, Connaught Place, New Delhi, Delhi 110001
  • Entry Fee:
    • 15 per person for Indians
    • 200 per person for Foreign Tourists
    • 15 per person for SAARC Visitors
    • 15 per person for BIMSTEC Visitors
